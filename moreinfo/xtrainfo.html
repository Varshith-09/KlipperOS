Klipper is a 3D printer firmware that's rapidly gaining popularity due to its high performance and flexibility. It's designed to provide a more responsive and efficient printing experience compared to traditional firmwares like Marlin.

How does a Klipper work?

Unlike traditional firmwares that run on the printer's microcontroller, Klipper operates on a separate, more powerful computer. This allows for more complex calculations and tasks, resulting in faster print times and smoother movements. The communication between the computer and the printer is handled through a dedicated interface, ensuring a low-latency connection.

Advantages Disadvantages
HighPerformance: Klipper's architecture allows for faster print speeds, acceleration, and deceleration. This results in shorter print times and improved print quality. Steeper Learning Curve: Setting up and configuring Klipper can be more complex than traditional firmwares. Users may need to invest more time and effort to get started.
AdvancedFeatures: Klipper supports a wide range of advanced features, including adaptive leveling, coasting, and voltage compensation. These features can help to improve print quality and reduce layer adhesion issues. Hardware Requirements: Klipper requires a separate computer or Raspberry Pi to run.
